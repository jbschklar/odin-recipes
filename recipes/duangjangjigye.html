Duangjang Jigye

Description

This is one of my favorite recipes from my time in Korea. This dish can be made with different ingredients but the base is always the same. This is a stew made with fermented soybean paste and varying seafood and vegetable ingredients. This is a version I've come to love but you can explore different varieties on your own.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup medium potato, peeled and cut into ½-inch cubes
  • 1 cup diced onion
  • 1 cup zucchini
  • 4 garlic cloves minced
  • 1/3 cup chopped shrimp
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • 7 dried anchovies
  • 5 tbsp fermented soybean paste (duangjang)
  • 6 oz medium-firm tofu, cut into ½-inch cubes
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Steps

  1. Combine potato, onion, zucchini, garlic, and shrimp in a large pot.
  2. Wrap the dried anchovies in cheesecloth and put them into the pot with other ingredients, add water and cover.
  3. Cook on medium heat until boiling.
  4. Stir in the soybean paste, cover and cook for 20 more minutes on medium heat.
  5. Add the tofu and cook for 3 more minutes. Remove the anchovy pouch and discard.
  6. Sprinkle with the green onions and serve as a side dish to ricein a soup bowl.
